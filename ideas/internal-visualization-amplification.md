# Internal Visualization Amplification

## Premise

Mental imagery is bottlenecked by reverse-direction bandwidth in the visual hierarchy. The dense feedforward projections from V1 to IT were built for perception; the projections that carry imagery in the opposite direction are sparser, slower, and noisier, and the gap between them is what makes imagining harder than seeing. Aphantasia and the wide variation in vividness across neurotypical subjects are consistent with this being an architectural choke point, not a training problem. An interface that writes directly to the higher levels of the visual hierarchy — inferotemporal cortex for object identity, parietal cortex for spatial relations, lateral occipital cortex for 3D shape — bypasses the bottleneck for the workloads that use imagery as a manipulation surface rather than as a felt re-perception. The amplification is not new content, more working memory, or new substrate. It is reading and writing visual representations at the levels where the work actually happens, without paying the cost of pushing them backwards through a pipeline whose backward direction was an afterthought.

## Mechanism

The visual hierarchy is asymmetric. V1, V2, V4, IT, and the dorsal parietal stream are connected by dense feedforward projections that carry bottom-up evidence in milliseconds. The reverse projections that instantiate imagery, expectation, and attention are an order of magnitude sparser. Aphantasia is consistent with weak top-down drive specifically at V1: higher visual areas are intact, but the felt-vividness that signals successful top-down propagation is absent. The relevant empirical anchor is that aphantasic subjects perform within range of controls on most mental-rotation and spatial-reasoning tasks. The phenomenology of imagery and the functional output of imagery dissociate.

The interface places dense write capacity at the apex levels of the hierarchy and deliberately skips V1. IT cortex hosts instantiations of objects and learned categories in its category-tuned columns. The lateral occipital complex represents 3D shape independent of viewpoint. Parietal cortex hosts spatial relations and mental-rotation circuits. The parahippocampal place area carries scene structure. A read channel from PFC, calibrated against the user's own task-vocabulary, decodes the user's manipulation intent — *rotate this protein*, *fold this manifold*, *walk through this building* — at the granularity of which object and which transform. The interface then writes the corresponding pattern into the relevant higher-level columns. The protein appears in IT as the object pattern and in parietal cortex as the rotation handle, without an intervening retinotopic image in V1.

The asymmetry is intentional. Reads from V1 continue to drive perception. Native top-down still produces ordinary imagery for content the user generates themselves. The interface only writes above V1 and only when the user has signaled an intent to manipulate something. The native imagery system runs in parallel, untouched and atrophiable.

Two regimes. Static instantiation writes a representation that persists for the duration of working memory; the user operates on it through ordinary parietal manipulations. Streaming instantiation updates the pattern in real time as PFC intent shifts, so the representation flows under directed manipulation — the protein rotates as the user reasons about its binding pockets, the manifold folds as the proof step requires. Streaming is the interesting regime; static is the simpler primitive to validate.

## Composition

Composes with direction 4 (concept-level IO): a transmitted activation pattern lands at IT and parietal cortex as an instantiated representation the receiver can manipulate, without the receiver having to push the pattern backwards through their own imagery pipeline first. Composes with direction 3 (random-access memory): a saved checkpoint includes the higher-visual-area state, restored on retrieval without regeneration cost. Composes with [[cognitive-telemetry]] and [[direct-introspection]] — both supply the PFC-intent read the interface needs to know what to instantiate. Without that read, the interface either runs continuously on whatever PFC happens to be doing, or it requires explicit user invocation, and neither preserves the felt-immediacy that makes imagery useful in the first place.

The boundary against direction 7 (extra cortex) is structural rather than rhetorical. Direction 7 grows new tissue and integrates it; the amplification is *more substrate*. This interface uses the user's existing tissue and reroutes input into it; the amplification is *bypassing a bandwidth choke in the native pipeline*. A mathematician with extra cortex has more parietal substrate available for spatial manipulation. A mathematician with internal visualization amplification has the same parietal substrate but no longer waits on top-down bandwidth to populate it. The two compose without overlap: a user with both has more substrate and better routing into it. The closer adjacency is to [[prior-surgery]], which also writes into the visual hierarchy, but prior surgery edits expectation, not content. An edited prior changes what the cortex predicts; an instantiated representation is the content the cortex is operating on. Different write surfaces.

The through-line extends: top-down imagery bottleneck → direct write to the working visual levels.

## What would falsify it

- If higher-level visual representations are not interpretable to downstream parietal and PFC consumers without simultaneous V1 activation — the brain's category and spatial circuits silently require retinotopic grounding for the representations they read above to mean anything — then bypassing V1 produces patterns the rest of the brain treats as noise, and the interface fails at the boundary it tries to cross.
- If aphantasic subjects show systematic deficits on the tasks the interface aims to support (mental rotation under varied transforms, novel-viewpoint scene construction, abstract spatial reasoning), then V1-mediated felt-vividness is functionally load-bearing for these tasks and not merely phenomenological. The bypass sacrifices function along with vividness.
- If category-tuned columns in IT and spatial circuits in parietal cortex are not addressable at the resolution required to disambiguate similar representations — *this protein conformation* versus *that protein conformation* smear across the same columns at scales below what any plausible interface can target — then writes are coarse and the user receives ambiguous content they cannot manipulate precisely.
- If higher visual areas require periodic activation through native top-down pathways to maintain their column tuning, and direct write bypasses the maintenance signal, then the addressed columns drift over months and the interface accelerates the degradation of its own substrate.
- If the latency from PFC-intent decode to higher-visual-area instantiation cannot reach the tens-of-milliseconds regime native imagery operates in, the interface is asynchronous in a way that breaks felt-immediacy. The working surface becomes a request-response system rather than an extension of cognition, and the use case collapses into something closer to manipulating a CAD program with thought.
- If the brain's authorship signal for imagery is generated specifically by native top-down routing — content arriving at IT without having passed through native pathways is tagged not-mine by source-monitoring circuits — then instantiated representations sit in higher visual areas unintegrated, and the user experiences them as alien presentations rather than usable working content.

## Open questions

- What does *vivid* mean when V1 is not engaged? The user may experience instantiated representations as a felt-knowing without a visual quality — closer to the abstract sense of an idea than to seeing it in the mind's eye. Whether this matters depends on the workload. Problem-solving may be agnostic to phenomenology; consolidation into long-term memory or transfer back to recognition may not be.
- Can the same parietal columns host instantiations from multiple domains simultaneously, or does the interface need to time-slice between, say, protein folding and architectural design? If the columns are interference-prone, the interface becomes a scheduler with context-switch costs and the per-session capability ceiling is set by tuning conflicts, not bandwidth.
- Does manipulating an instantiated representation require motor cortex involvement, as native mental rotation does? If the bypass decouples imagery from motor-imagery circuits, the user may need a new control pathway, and the interface effectively introduces a new manipulation primitive rather than amplifying the existing one.
- What happens to native imagery after years of interface use? If the top-down pathways atrophy through disuse, unmediated imagery degrades to a baseline below the starting point.
- Is there a class of imagery tasks where felt-vividness is itself the load-bearing variable rather than the functional output — exposure-based fear-extinction, narrative engagement, episodic re-experiencing — and what is the user to do when the bypass works for problem-solving but cannot reach the use cases that need vividness?

## Risks

- Phenomenological hollow. Instantiated representations may be functionally equivalent to native imagery but feel different: less owned, less integrated, less felt-as-mine. The user gets the work done and reports that the imagery is not theirs in the way native imagery is. This may be aesthetically inert, or it may matter for downstream cognition that depends on imagery being authored rather than received. The honest position is that the experiment has not been run because the bypass cannot be performed today. Aphantasia is the closest natural analogue, and aphantasic problem-solving is unimpaired but distinct in introspective character — which is the answer the interface should expect to inherit.
- Skill atrophy through bypass. Native top-down pathways are exercised by the act of imagining. An interface that instantiates patterns the user would have constructed by effort removes the training signal, and the unmediated imagery system degrades. Years of use produce a user who cannot reason visually without the interface, where they could before.
- Source-attribution failure. Patterns arriving at IT and parietal cortex without having passed through native imagery routing lack the source signature the brain uses to mark content as imagined-by-me. The user is at elevated risk of mistaking instantiated content for perceived content, or for the memory of perceived content. Composition with [[source-tagged-cognition]] becomes mandatory rather than optional.
- Authorship dilution. When a chess master visualizes a position, they are reasoning *through* the imagery; the imagery is part of the cognition. When the interface instantiates the position from PFC intent, the boundary between *user reasoning under instantiated imagery* and *user reading the interface's rendering* is not bright. Over time the user may not be able to say which they were doing, and the question of who solved the problem becomes harder to answer. The bead's intuition that shape (B) — external rendering loop — slides toward substitution rather than amplification applies here too in weaker form: the bypass keeps inference inside the user's brain, but the supplied content is not authored there.
- Adversarial instantiation. The write channel that delivers a folded protein on user intent delivers other content on attacker intent. The defensive surface is qualitatively new: a lifetime of trusting imagery as one's own does not equip the user to mark instantiated content as foreign, because the felt-quality the user would normally rely on for that judgment is exactly what the bypass routes around.
- Selective bypass as creeping replacement. The interface starts as a working surface for chosen tasks. Each task that succeeds under instantiation removes a reason to use native imagery. The fraction of cognition routed through the bypass grows by attrition rather than by decision. After enough time, the user has rebuilt visual reasoning around the interface's affordances, and a question that would have been natural before — *what does this look like in your mind?* — has no answer the user recognizes.
